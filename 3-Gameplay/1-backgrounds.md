# Backgrounds, Resources & Systems

-----

This document outlines how [CHRONICLE NAME] handles Backgrounds mechanically and narratively, including resource access, interactions with the environment, and any chronicle-specific rulings.

Each Background entry should define the following:

- **How it functions in your chronicle**
- **What level(s) unlock which features**
- **Any staff approval, roleplay, or item requirements**
- **If it is stackable or capped locally**
- **Special uses in downtime or in scenes**

## General Background Guidelines

- Players must note all active Backgrounds on their character sheet.
- Any Background that requires physical representation (cards, tokens, items) must be present at check-in or carried at game.
- Backgrounds **do not substitute for roleplay**. They enhance it.

**Stacking Method Example (Laws of Ascension):**
1. Start with the highest Background rating.
2. Add half the value (rounded down) of each additional identical Background.
3. If the total equals or exceeds the base rating, increase the effective value by 1.
4. Repeat until the total no longer permits an increase.

## Suggested Backgrounds to Include

Use the above template format for each of the following (as applicable to your setting):

- **Allies** – Personal contacts who help out when asked.
- **Alternate Identity** – Legal identities that withstand scrutiny.
- **Arcane** – Supernatural difficulty in being noticed or tracked.
- **Contacts** – Informal sources in various areas of society.
- **Cult** – Followers who believe in the character or a cause.
- **Domain/Territory** – Controlled land with narrative or mechanical protections.
- **Fame** – Public recognition; may assist or complicate social encounters.
- **Herd** – Mortal blood sources for vampires or similar feeding groups.
- **Influence** – Control over institutions (media, law, police, etc.).
- **Library/Occult** – Access to rare knowledge, tomes, and lore.
- **Mentor** – Guidance and assistance from a more experienced NPC.
- **Resources** – Financial wealth and liquid assets.
- **Retainers** – NPCs who serve the character in various capacities.
- **Status** – Recognition within a faction, clan, court, or group.
- **Transportation** – Access to vehicles, ships, or supernatural movement methods.
- **Workshop/Laboratory** – Location with tools for crafting or research.

## Example (DO NOT COPY — FOR REFERENCE ONLY)

### Resources

**Narrative Role:**  
Measures wealth, income, and access to money or material goods.

**Mechanical Function:**  
Used to acquire gear, bribe NPCs, or represent lifestyle. Collection must be declared each downtime or game session.

**Level Effects:**
- **x0:** Subsistence living; $400/month.
- **x1:** Basic income; $1,000/month.
- **x2:** Comfortable; $2,000/month.
- **x3:** Middle-class affluence; $6,000/month.
- **x4:** Wealthy; $20,000/month.
- **x5:** Opulent; $60,000/month.
- **x6+:** Exceptional (ST approval required).

**Chronicle Notes:**
- Players must have a **Resources card** to spend from this Background.
- Retroactive collection is not allowed.
- STs may adjust for inflation or downtime markets.

### Mentor

**Narrative Role:**  
A powerful or experienced figure guiding the character’s growth.

**Mechanical Function:**  
May provide training, warnings, rare items, or social leverage.

**Level Effects:**
- **x1:** Occasional advice or hints.
- **x2:** Minor assistance once per session.
- **x3:** May pull strings or provide short-term access to powers.
- **x4:** Influential figure with agency in their faction.
- **x5:** Renowned or feared presence who actively influences plots.

**Chronicle Notes:**
- Mentor must be established as a named NPC.
- Staff may use or target Mentors as plot tools.
